Volume method: 4 sets per exercise · Stop 2–3 reps short of failure · Max 10 sets per muscle per session · Rest 60–90 sec between sets
Workouts
Upper body
~55–65 min
8 sets chest · 8 sets back · 4 sets delts
Bench press
Chest — primary push
4 × 8–12-2 reps▾
Stop when you have 2–3 reps left in the tank. 4 sets here + 4 incline = 8 chest sets today. Add weight when you hit 12 on all 4 sets easily.
Incline dumbbell press
Upper chest
4 × 10–12-2 reps▾
Completes 8 chest sets. Control the negative (3 sec down). Upper chest is underdeveloped in most beginners — don't skip this.
Lat pulldown
Lats — primary pull
4 × 8–12-2 reps▾
Shoulder-width grip, slight lean back. Drive elbows down toward hips. 4 sets here + 4 rows = 8 back sets today.
Machine row
Upper back + rear delts
4 × 10–12-2 reps▾
Pronated grip, squeeze 1 sec at top. Completes 8 back sets. Machine is ideal for beginners since the path is fixed.
Lateral raises
Side delts
4 × 12–15-2 reps▾
Cable or dumbbell. Lead with elbows, slight bend. Fastest way to build shoulder width. 4 here + 4 Friday = 8 weekly sets.
Lower body
~55–65 min
8 sets quads · 8 sets hamstrings · 4 sets calves
Barbell squat
Quads + glutes
4 × 8–10-2 reps▾
Stop 2–3 reps short — Jeremy specifically recommends this for squats since true failure on legs tanks recovery. Full depth, knees track over toes.
Leg press
Quads — supplemental
4 × 10–12-2 reps▾
Completes 8 quad sets. Full range — let the sled come close to your chest. Safer than squats for volume since there's no spinal load.
Romanian deadlift
Hamstrings + glutes
4 × 8–10-2 reps▾
Jeremy's top hamstring exercise. Push hips back, feel the stretch. Keep bar close to your legs. Stop 2–3 short — RDL near failure is risky for the lower back.
Lying leg curl
Hamstrings — isolation
4 × 10–15-2 reps▾
Completes 8 hamstring sets. Slow negative (3 sec). Safe to push closer to failure here — no spinal load.
Standing calf raise
Calves
4 × 15–20-2 reps▾
Deep stretch at the bottom, full contraction at top. High reps work best for calves. 4 here + 4 Friday = 8 weekly sets.
Full body
~60–70 min
Tops off weekly volume for every muscle
Overhead press
Shoulders + triceps
4 × 8–10-2 reps▾
Brings shoulders to 8 weekly sets (4 lateral Mon + 4 here). Core tight, don't flare elbows.
Pull-up / assisted pull-up
Lats + biceps
4 × 8–12-2 reps▾
Brings back to 12 weekly sets (8 Mon + 4 here). Use assisted machine if needed. When you hit 4×12 bodyweight, add a plate.
Hack squat or leg press
Quads
4 × 10–12-2 reps▾
Brings quads to 12 weekly sets (8 Wed + 4 here). Jeremy's top quad pick is the Cybex hack squat — use it if your gym has one.
Dumbbell skull crushers
Triceps
4 × 10–12-2 reps▾
Jeremy's top tricep exercise. Elbows stay pointed at ceiling, slow negative. Triceps = 2/3 of arm size.
Cable or dumbbell curl
Biceps
4 × 10–12-2 reps▾
Full range — all the way down, squeeze at top. Try Bayesian curls (cable behind you) for maximum bicep stretch.
Calf raise
Calves
4 × 15–20-2 reps▾
Brings calves to 8 weekly sets (4 Wed + 4 here). Full range every rep — partial reps are almost useless for calves.

Recomp hack: ~200 kcal below maintenance on rest days (2,000 kcal). Closer to maintenance on training days (2,200 kcal). Calorie cycling per Jeremy Ethier.
Jeremy Ethier's 5 rules
1
Hit protein first — everything else is secondary.
~165g/day. Protein protects muscle while cutting fat.
2
Eat the same 3–4 meals on repeat.
Decision fatigue kills consistency. Rotate weekly if bored.
3
Whole foods 80% of the time.
20% flexible. Rigid diets fail. Flexible ones stick.
4
Time carbs around workouts.
Most carbs before/after training. Better performance + recovery.
5
Track for 2 weeks, then go by feel.
Build awareness once — you won't need to track forever.
